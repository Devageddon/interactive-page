Executive Summary

UNB invites DP World to join forces in an alliance centred around innovative education, new solutions, and the supply chain management and logistics industry. In a responsive relationship, DP World and UNB will co-create an agenda for education and knowledge transfer from research to application, so that UNB experts take action to address the issues of today, while generating the solutions of tomorrow. Investment by DP World in UNB will set the foundations for long-term sustainable innovation and collaboration across a breadth of university activities. Each investment area can be pursued in isolation or combination. The total value of the partnership is $10 million over 5 years.

This investment may encompass some or all of the following areas:

1. Education Network

  • Minor and major pathways in supply chain management and logistics through credited degree programs at UNB, an established Canadian public university;
  • Experiential and work-integrated learning to prepare learners with the hands-on skills needed in the workforce;
  • Professional and executive education and to respond to the evolving needs of the sector;
  • Micro-credentials co-branded by UNB and DP World to further professionalize the sector.

2. Supply Chain Management Collaboration Hub

  • Network of innovation involving DP World, industry partners, community, and world-leading researchers;
  • Generate out-of-the-box thinkers by supporting research in the area of supply chain management and logistics;
  • Problem Solving Summits to facilitate knowledge sharing and collaborative ideation.

3. Infrastructural Legacy Project

  • Unite the legacies of DP World, UNB, and the community of Saint John by investing in a major infrastructure project.

Introduction

New Brunswick boasts a rich history of manufacturing, including everything from chocolates to fish, French fries, lumber and oil products. This variety of industry, and the province’s diverse population, multiplicity of residential types, and excellence in technological innovation make it fertile ground for research and development.

The beautiful city of Saint John solidifies this status, connecting the region to international trade routes through Port Saint John on sea, within 25km of a variety of land assets, as well as a 1.5h distance to the Greater Moncton Roméo LeBlanc International Airport (YQM), an international air cargo facility.

Adding to the richness of the province and city, the University of New Brunswick is Canada’s oldest English language university. UNB has an enduring reputation of excellence in education and research, and a proven track record of collaboration with impact. Its Saint John campus, with locations in both the city centre and Port Saint John, has a reputation for its industry connections; for staying up to date with contemporary issues, to predict solutions, and to lead in applied education and research

A Case Study in Success

Education to shape industry

Recognizing the importance of teaching and learning opportunities to meet the needs of a variety of markets, UNB launched the Centre for Learning Futures with a mandate of creating transformational learning experiences for learners of all types. UNB is proud to support our learners with their academic pursuits, executive or professional development needs or personal development goals.

We understand that learners have different needs and have a proven track-record of meeting learners where they are. Our programming spans academic, executive, professional development, creative, cultural, and linguistic pursuits. We operate online, in person, hybrid and hyflex, and our learners gain credit and non-credit leaving awards, including microcredentials, certificates, diplomas and degrees.

With this dynamic model, we present a truly customizable experience and produce transformative results. Moreover, we remain nimble and able to respond to the changing needs of industry through flexible and multifaceted educational programming.

  1. Leading the way in work-integrated learning

    In 2024, forty UNB students worked with the Port Saint John Employers Association and Canada’s Ocean Supercluster to explore sustainable oceanic economic growth. The students created entrepreneurial concepts, mapped systems and analyzed stakeholder dynamics.

  2. Unforgettable opportunities for growth

    The annual Student Pitch Competition was sponsored by Royal Bank of Canada in 2025. Diverse students receive feedback from industry judges on a business proposal, network with professionals, and develop lifelong leadership and communication skills.

  3. 100,000+ alumni globally

    Craig Bell Estabrooks, President and CEO Port Saint John, is one of the 100,000+ UNB alumni making positive change in the global community. Like many other graduates, Estabrooks shares an enduring fondness for the roots set down through a UNB education.


Research to change industry

1. Research Institute in Data Science and Artificial Intelligence (RIDSAI)

Established in 2023, as a result of a $3.5 million investment by donors including Mr. Frank McKenna (former premier of NB and benefactor of UNB’s McKenna Institute), RIDSAI is home to leading UNB experts in the research, application and impact of data science and artificial intelligence.

Dr. Aaloak Jaswal, Director of Innovation RIDSAI, a leader in digital transformation and innovation, has provided technical and policy advice to UN organizations, government counterparts, and relevant partners in the technology and public health sectors. Most recently, he served as Head of Digital Transformation for the UN Economic Commission for Africa (UNECA), where he consistently drove impactful change in complex, global environments.


2. UNB Ocean Mapping Group

Established in 1991, this group is focused on developing new and innovative techniques and tools for the management, processing, visualization and interpretation of ocean mapping data.

UNB’s Ocean Mapping Group are involved in Seabed 2030, a collaboration between GEBCO and Nippon Foundation to map the entire ocean floor by 2030. Valued at over US$18.5 million for the first ten years, Seabed 2030 aims to compile bathymetric data into a high-resolution digital model and promote new data collection efforts.


3. Emera & NB Power Research Centre for Smart Grid Technologies

Established in 2017, with a $6.2 million investment from Emera, this Centre is a global leader in the transformation of electric power systems into smart grids through R&D, innovation, commercial partnerships, high-quality researchers and state-of-the-art research facilities.

Through the Emera & NB Power Research Centre for Smart Grid Technologies, UNB became a founding partner of the Smart Grid Innovation Network (SGIN) alongside Siemens Canada. SGIN fosters the co-creation and testing of cutting edge of smart grid technology.

We will work with DP World to identify existing programming opportunities. We will work together in a collaborative space to develop new, customized programs.

UNB has a history of creating customized programs to meet the needs of business. For example, on the academic side, the UNB Saint John MBA developed a customized MBA program for Irving Oil that combined academic training with experiential learning opportunities within the company. Over 150 Irving Oil Employees went through this program. Mike Aashar, the General Manager at the time spoke to the success of the program in two ways. First having one of the most highly trained workforces in the industry based on parentage of employees and second, having covered the tuition costs in monies saved in consultant fees by having employees work with managers on company issues as the experiential component of their degree program.

Opportunities exist for those entering our credit certificate, diploma and degree programs to apply for Prior Learning Assessment. This means that their work experience and other skills can give them advanced standing in an academic program of their choosing, which reduces the number of courses they need to take in their chosen program.

To respond to the changing needs of learners, and to reach geographically dispersed populations, UNB offers over 100 online credit courses and has expanded into fully-online programs.

Our online courses vary in delivery style.

In addition to academic programming UNB also offers both executive education and professional development programming and has received recognition for many of its program initiatives.

For example: UNB offers a non-credit Occupational Health and Safety Certificate that meets the formal education requirements for certification as a Canadian Registered Safety Technician. If completed along with our Diploma in Occupational Health and Safety, learners will have fulfilled the educational hours to write Canadian Registered Safety Professional (CRSP) exam. UNB’s non-credit certificate in Safety Leadership counts toward continuous learning points for those with designations and an Ethics for Safety Leaders Program that meets the requirements for BCRSP’s mandatory ethics course for BCRSP.

UNB is committed to education that transcends boundaries and borders. No matter where our learners find themselves, we are dedicated to their transformational growth.

Considering the current challenges and the many opportunities for innovation and growth that lie ahead for DP World, we propose the creation of a Supply Chain Management Collaboration Hub. While the name is subject to change, the Hub will be a uniquely designed collaboration framework focused on adding value across DP World’s priorities of terminal operations, innovation, and logistics.

The Supply Chain Management Collaboration Hub will foster open and frank discussion and debate between stakeholders.

UNB’s leadership will coordinate and consult with DP World to tackle your greatest challenges.

UNB understands the value of a collaborative approach for problem solving and big sky thinking. With maximizing impact as the core deliverable, having the right minds around the table takes on even more significance.

The Global Supply Chain Collaboration Hub will use several local locations (Port Saint John Fundy Room, UNB MBA Building uptown Saint John, UNB campuses in Saint John, Fredericton, and Moncton) as determined by the resources required, problem being solved, or partners participating. Capitalizing on UNB’s diverse real-estate assets around the province, as well as UNB’s new dedicated presence on Port Saint John property, will present the immediate opportunity to create a different kind of innovation mind space, focused on creating collaborative teams to tackle challenges, identify opportunities and work together to define a path forward with growth and impact as the goals.

Our collaborative approach to accessing expertise, information and thought leadership as opposed to formal consulting services, fostering unfettered communication channels impervious to physical location is what sets us apart in the education space. This environment allows for the intersection of knowledge, skills, experience, interest and passion with opportunity, challenge, need, and unanticipated events allowing us to question what is, what could be and what should be as we explore innovative solutions that will shape the future.

UNB will act as the aggregator that brings together the right people, ideas and skills to address the issues of the moment, and to help DP World predict the solutions needed for the future.
